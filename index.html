About Me

My name is Khalid Hamu, and I come from Denver, Colorado. My interests revolve around various fields, including computers, art, animation, design, and engineering. My long-term goal is to combine some of my passions into my career and projects. Currently, I am pursuing a Computer Science major and a Digital Design minor at the University of Colorado Denver. I expect to graduate in the Spring of 2024 and aspire to work as a Software Engineer. While I am passionate about subjects outside of my major and minor, for now, they remain hobbies.

The pandemic brought about a significant shift in me, and I now view the world through a different lens. With the luxury of time, I was able to explore my interests in greater detail. Prior to the pandemic, I would have classified myself as someone who had no inclination towards creative pursuits, such as art or design. However, as the world seemed to slow down, I found myself with more time to reflect on my passions. I started exploring the digital design world through online tutorials and was amazed at how much I enjoyed creating graphics and animations. As I became more confident in my abilities, I realized that I wanted to pursue a minor in Digital Design.


This decision was not straightforward. Growing up, I was always drawn to math and logic, and I saw art and creativity as a separate realm that I didn't have a natural aptitude for. Yet, as I delved deeper into digital design, I discovered that creativity and logic are not mutually exclusive. I realized that designing requires both critical thinking and a keen eye for aesthetics, and it was fascinating to see how the two intersected. Moreover, I discovered that my newfound appreciation for digital design has influenced how I approach problem-solving in my computer science classes. I have begun to apply the design thinking process to my coding projects, which has resulted in more user-friendly interfaces and streamlined workflows. This cross-disciplinary approach has been rewarding, and I am excited to see where it takes me in the future.

Coding Projects

Pet Shop Database

In designing and implementing the pet shop database, I took into consideration the requirements of the system. This involved analyzing the different business processes, as well as understanding the needs of the end-users. I also ensured that the database was properly normalized to avoid data redundancy and inconsistencies, and that the queries were optimized. Using PHP and HTML, I was able to create a user-friendly web interface that allowed users to interact with the database and access information easily, which in turn helped improve the overall functionality and usability of the pet shop system.

Bank Account Monthly Statement

The program I developed is designed to take in CSV files representing bank statements with corresponding dates, and generate a summary of each month for each account. I implemented appropriate data structures and algorithms in Python to accurately parse the CSV files, and wrote optimized SQL queries to extract and summarize the data. Through the use of Python's data visualization libraries, I created user-friendly outputs that made it easy to access and interpret the generated summaries, making it an effective tool for account reconciliation and financial management.
